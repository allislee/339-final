Meal Recipes!

restaurant

These are some of my favorite recipes for some healthy but delicious meals! These dishes all have ingredients low on the glycemic index, so they are a great option for anyone looking to manage blood sugar levels.

Tomato Egg

Tomato egg stir fry is a very popular dish in Taiwanese and Chinese cuisine. It is very simple and quick to make and is a personal favorite of mine; whenever I am in a rush or want something quick but delicious to eat, this is my go to! I have cooked it so many times this past semester, and it has been super yummy each time! Sometimes I eat it with rice and sometimes I don't; if you are watching blood sugar levels, definitely try to pass on the rice!

Ingredients!

  • Eggs
  • Tomatoes
  • Garlic
  • Green Onion
  • Soy Sauce
  • Rice (Optional)

Some tomato egg I made during the semetser!

Tomato Health Benefits!
Tomatoes are low on the glycemic index and are not high in total carbohydrates or sugar like many other fruits or vegetables. They are also high in vitamins like A, C, E, and K!
Egg Health Benefits!
Eggs are a good source of protein and can therefore be a good alternative to other high fat and carbohydrate protein sources like certain meats for those with high blood sugar. However, they can be a potent source of cholesterol, so it is important to not consumes eggs in excess!

Steamed Tofu

Steamed Tofu is a super simple but yummy dish! This is a perfect dish for those that are vegetarian and looking for a simple meal to enjoy. It is soft, simple, but full of flavor and one of my favorites!

Ingredients!

  • Tofu
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Chili Peppers
  • Oyster Sauce
  • Soy Sauce
Tofu Health Benefits!
Tofu is not only super high in protein, but it is also very low on the glycemic index, making it a very safe food option for people with high blood sugar. Tofu is made from soybeans, which are very high in healthy fibers and isoflavones which have been strongly associated with numerous health benefits.
Garlic and Onion Health Information!
Garlic and onion have also long been shown to confer many antidiabetic, antiangiogenesis, and neuroprotective effects, so add a lot of garlic and onion to your meals!

Bok Choy and Mushrooms

Stir fried bokchoy and mushrooms is a very flavorful and enjoyable meal! Bokchoy is one of my favorite vegetables and can be used in a lot of different ways. The sweetness of the sauce paired along with the bokchoy and vegetables make it a super delicious meal!

Ingredients!

  • Bok Choy
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Brown Mushrooms
  • Oyster Sauce
  • Soy Sauce
  • Ginger
  • Black Pepper
Bokchoy Health Information!
Bok choy, like many other leafy vegetables, are high in quercetin, which can help to reduce inflammation and decrease chances of developing heart disease, diabetes, and cancer. Bokchoy is also high in vitamin A, which is good for your skin too!
Mushroom Health Information!
Mushrooms are a safe food for diabetics, as they are low on the glycemic index, and their high vitamin content can help to improve blood sugar levels and glucose levels!
